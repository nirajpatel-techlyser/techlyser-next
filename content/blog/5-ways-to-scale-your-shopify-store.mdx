Running a successful Shopify store means looking at scaling and growth! Here’s how…



### **#1 Work on Your Expertise**



Being an expert in your niche is what will bring more customers and large-scale Shopify Store growth.



Getting to the expert level is not easy, nor is staying there. You must be active and follow industry trends, and know what matters to your customers too.



Find out where your customers like to frequent online and be there. Offer answers to questions they are asking and provide useful tips as well as advice to help them make the right decisions.

### **#3 Adjust Your Business Model To Handle Growth**



The hardest part of scaling is knowing when it’s time to stop with certain activities and focus on the ones that deliver results.



If a product line you started on isn’t performing well anymore, it might be time to cancel the line. See which products and services bring revenue, and which are costing you more than they earn you.



Adjusting your whole model requires you to adjust your eCommerce model plan too. You cannot have a large-scale Shopify Store without a plan that can support your growth.   This covers everything from supply to shipping and seasonal sales.



### **#4 Fill the Gap**



An increase in sales won’t happen without identifying a gap to fill. Find something that is missing in your niche and see what you can do to make it better.



Customer experience is often a way to stand out from the crowd. A great experience for customers, one that is much better than what your competitors are having, is a way to achieve this.



Happy customers bring more revenue—both through repeat orders and bringing in more customers through word of mouth.



### **#5 Find Lessons in Other Niches**



You might think that you won’t find anything useful from those in other industries in regard to how to move forward if you’re stuck, but all industries have certain touchpoints.



Beauty products might not have much to do with automotive parts, but identifying low-performing lines and deciding what to do with them will follow the same rules.



Exchange stories about struggles and successes with others, and you might just find a solution for your current issue.
